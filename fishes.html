Meet the Deep-Sea Predators

Anglerfish

Anglerfish (Order Lophiiformes) use a modified dorsal fin ray like a fishing rod to lure prey in the darkness of the deep sea.

Gulper Eel

The Gulper Eel (Eurypharynx pelecanoides) has a giant, hinged mouth that can swallow prey larger than itself.

Viperfish

Viperfish (Chauliodus) live in the mesopelagic zone, known for their needle-like teeth and bioluminescence.

Lanternfish

Lanternfish (Family Myctophidae) are small but extremely abundant, making up to 65% of deep-sea fish biomass.

Dragonfish (Stomiidae)

Dragonfishes have enormous jaws with fangs and can open their mouths over 100°, allowing them to eat prey half their size.

Snailfish

Snailfish (Family Liparidae) thrive from shallow waters to depths over 8,300 m, including deep ocean trenches.

Did You Know?

Lanternfish may account for 16 gigatonnes of biomass worldwide!
Viperfish migrate vertically daily, chasing prey.
Dragonfish can consume prey 50% larger than their body length.
