What is eDNA?

Environmental DNA (eDNA) is genetic material shed by organisms into their environment. By collecting and analyzing eDNA from water samples, scientists can identify species present in an ecosystem without directly observing them. This revolutionizes biodiversity monitoring, especially in the vast and unexplored deep sea.
